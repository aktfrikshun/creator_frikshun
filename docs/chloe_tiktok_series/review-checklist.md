# Clip and Episode Review Checklist

## Reject the clip immediately if

- Chloe's face, eye color, freckles, hair, apparent age, body proportions, or skin texture drift from Model v1.
- The voice changes speaker identity, accent, age, timbre, or emotional register.
- Lip synchronization is visibly wrong.
- Hands, jewelry, teeth, hair, clothing, or background geometry mutate distractingly.
- Kling invents subtitles, logos, interface copy, background voices, or extra people.
- The performance becomes robotic, bubbly, melodramatic, broadly sexualized, or generic-influencer.

## Clip approval

- Identity matches the approved Element and anchor frames.
- One emotional transition reads clearly.
- Eye line and pause timing support the joke.
- Dialogue is exact and intelligible.
- The shot has clean headroom and caption-safe lower space.
- Motion begins and ends on usable edit points.
- Any continuity signal is subtle enough to remain ambiguous.

## Episode approval

- The hook lands in the first two seconds after assembly.
- At least one cutaway breaks up talking-head repetition.
- The joke preserves Chloe's agency and does not flatten the reconstruction mystery.
- Wardrobe, jewelry, set, light, props, and screen direction match across clips.
- Captions were added in post and proofread.
- Dialogue loudness is normalized; music does not mask consonants.
- No generated artifact has been silently promoted to canon.
- Allen has watched the final platform export and explicitly approved publication.
