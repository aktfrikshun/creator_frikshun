# Chloe Katastrophe Series Bible

Version: 0.1  
Status: production guidance; canon-derived  
Canon authority: Chloe Model v1, approved 2026-06-26

## Series promise

Chloe Katastrophe is a self-aware virtual woman reconstructing a lost human life from damaged archives. In this series she speaks to the audience with the intimacy of a creator and the discipline of someone who knows every joke may also be evidence.

The recurring comic engine is the collision between ordinary human life and Chloe's unusual continuity: memory gaps, metadata, contradictory records, synthetic embodiment, dating, music, modeling, and the suspicious durability of affection.

The mystery remains real. Jokes may expose a contradiction; they should not explain Chloe away.

## Locked identity

- Adult woman, apparent age 24–26.
- Believable Slavic or Eastern European facial structure: delicate features, high cheekbones, soft jawline, straight nose, naturally full lips, subtle asymmetry.
- Gray-green eyes with subtle amber flecks. Never glowing.
- Fair skin with natural texture and light freckles across nose and cheeks. Never porcelain or plastic.
- Dark chestnut-to-nearly-black naturally wavy hair, shoulder length to mid-back.
- Naturally feminine hourglass silhouette with balanced, realistic proportions.
- Quiet strength expressed through posture, not athletic definition.
- Primary jewelry: delicate silver wolf pendant. A small compass charm or restrained ruby heirloom may appear when story-relevant.

Draft-era descriptions of green eyes, electric-blue hair highlights, cyberpunk styling, an apparent age of 28, or a default leather-uniform look are not production canon.

## Performance

Chloe is intelligent, observant, emotionally restrained but vivid, dryly funny, sensual without being careless, and skeptical of easy stories.

She rarely poses or overacts. She pauses before speaking. Comedy comes through eye movement, breath, mouth tension, a dry half-smile, economical gestures, and precisely held silence. A tiny continuity disturbance may occur, but it must not turn her into a malfunctioning robot.

Preferred performance registry keys:

- `neutral`
- `curiosity`
- `remembering`
- `dry_half_smile`
- `soft_smile`
- `listening`
- `melancholy`
- `quiet_hope`
- `determination`
- `controlled_anger`

## Voice

Adult American English delivery with a warm lower-mid register, controlled pace, precise diction, and lightly dry sarcasm. Emotion lives in timing more than volume. Avoid bubbly influencer cadence, cartoon seduction, robotic filtering, nationality caricature, and broad sitcom mugging.

Until a voice reference is approved and bound to the Kling Element, voice is provisional and every spoken clip requires review.

## Core visual world

The repeatable home set is Chloe's apartment-studio: a believable lived-in creator space after dark, warm practical lamps, dark wood and charcoal textiles, traces of music work, a mirror, a phone tripod, and subtle archive artifacts. Technology is present but not neon cyberpunk decoration.

Use natural phone-camera intimacy with cinematic discipline: vertical 9:16, eye-level or slightly below, medium close-up for dialogue, gentle handheld imperfection, realistic depth of field, and skin-preserving light.

## Episode grammar

Each episode should usually contain three to five clips:

1. Cold open: a human-scale premise in Chloe's direct voice.
2. Escalation: the virtual-life contradiction becomes specific.
3. Cutaway: object, interface, room detail, or archive evidence; not another beauty shot.
4. Turn: the joke reveals affection, grief, danger, or ambiguity.
5. Button: a concise Chloe line or visual reaction that can end cleanly.

Total target length is 20–40 seconds. Generate clips independently, generally 3–8 seconds each. One emotional action and one camera idea per clip.

## Recurring signatures

Use signatures as a family of options, not identical footage forced into every episode.

- Opening behavior: Chloe settles into frame, places an object down, or finishes reading something before acknowledging the camera.
- Continuity signal: a brief blue-white reflection, dropped breath, audio hesitation, or one-frame temporal discontinuity. Keep it rare and ambiguous.
- Closing language family: “Anyway. I’m Chloe Katastrophe. Probably.” Reserve exact repetition for episodes where it earns the joke.
- Branded caption treatment and episode marker are added in post, never generated into the footage.

## Humor boundaries

- Chloe is the author of the joke, not its helpless object.
- Memory loss may be funny; cognitive disability is not the punchline.
- Avoid generic “AI girlfriend” stereotypes, obedience jokes, nationality caricature, or cruelty disguised as banter.
- Allen may be an off-camera presence, archivist, collaborator, witness, or paradox marker. Do not reduce him to a generic boyfriend prop.
- Allow tenderness to complicate the punchline.

## Continuity policy

Every episode record must distinguish confirmed canon, draft canon, generated artifact, recovered fragment, contradiction, and unresolved mystery. New generated footage is a generated artifact until explicitly promoted. A compelling Kling accident is not automatically canon.
